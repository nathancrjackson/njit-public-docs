The term \"Documented information\" is used within ISO 27001:2022 27 times. There is no one correct way to manage your documented information but the following are some key documents you would be expected to maintain.

<table class="collapse-1280">
<thead>
<tr><td style="min-width:100px">Document</td><td>Description</td><td>Notes</td></tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">ISMS Manual</td>
	<td>A document for defining the scope of the ISMS, who relevant stakeholders are, their needs, and who is responsible for what within the ISMS.</td>
	<td>
		<p>This is ideal for holding documented information pertaining to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 4: Context of the organization</li>
			<li>Clause 5.3: Organizational roles, responsibilities and authorities</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Information Security Policy</td>
	<td>A high-level document outlining the organization's approach to information security.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 5.2: Information Security Policy</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Risk Assessment and Treatment Methodology</td>
	<td>A defined process to ensure that information security risk assessments are consistent, valid, and comparable.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 6.1.2: Information security risk assessment</li>
			<li>Clause 6.1.3: Information security risk treatment</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Statement of Applicability (SoA)</td>
	<td>A document listing necessary controls, justifications for their inclusion or exclusion, and their current implementation status.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 6.1.3 d): Statement of Applicability</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Information Security Objectives</td>
	<td>Documented goals that are consistent with the Information Security Policy.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 6.2: Information security objectives</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Risk Assessment and Treatment Results</td>
	<td>Evidence that risk assessments and treatment plans have been performed and implemented as planned.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 8.2: Information security risk assessment</li>
			<li>Clause 8.3: Information security risk treatment</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Internal Audit Programme and Results</td>
	<td>Documentation of the audit schedule and evidence of the audit results to prove the ISMS is effectively maintained.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 9.2: Internal audit</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Management Review Results</td>
	<td>Evidence of the outcomes from top management's reviews of the ISMS, including decisions on improvements.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 9.3: Management review</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
<tr>
	<td class="bold-1280">Nonconformity and Corrective Action Logs</td>
	<td>Records detailing the nature of nonconformities, actions taken to correct them, and the results of those actions.</td>
	<td>
		<p>Pertains to:</p>
		<ul>
			<li>Clause 10.2: Nonconformity and corrective action</li>
		</ul>
	</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
