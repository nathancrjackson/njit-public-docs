PRINCE2 is a widely used project management method that provides a structured approach to managing projects by dividing it into seven processes. It emphasizes the importance of clear objectives, defined roles and responsibilities, and a focus on progress monitoring and control throughout the project lifecycle.

PRINCE2, an acronym for **PR**ojects **IN** **C**ontrolled **E**nvironments, is a globally recognized, structured project management methodology. It provides a process-based approach designed to enhance organization and control within projects, focusing on the effective management of resources and risks. Central to PRINCE2 is its emphasis on product-based planning, meaning the methodology centres on the definition, delivery, and quality of specific project outputs or 'products'. These products must meet clearly defined quality criteria and contribute to achieving a justified business case, ensuring the project delivers tangible value.

PRINCE2 is designed as a generic framework, making it applicable and adaptable to a wide variety of projects, irrespective of their scale, complexity, type, organizational context, geographical location, or culture. This versatility has led to its widespread adoption across numerous industries and sectors worldwide, including notable use within the UK public sector (such as the NHS and Home Office), the IT sector, construction, and engineering.

The PRINCE2 methodology is built upon three integrated core components:

- **7 Principles:** The fundamental guiding obligations and best practices.  
- **7 Practices:** Aspects of project management that must be continually addressed throughout the project. (Formerly referred to as themes)
- **7 Processes:** A series of steps outlining the project lifecycle activities.

These elements work together, providing a comprehensive framework that can be tailored to suit the specific needs of any project. While providing a robust structure, PRINCE2 is not intended to be applied rigidly. The principle of **Tailoring** is fundamental, requiring the methodology to be adapted to the specific size, complexity, risk, and environment of each unique project to ensure efficiency and effectiveness. When applied appropriately, PRINCE2 offers significant benefits, including improved communication through a common language, enhanced governance and control, better risk management, and ultimately, an increased likelihood of delivering projects successfully, meeting stakeholder expectations, and achieving the intended business benefits.
