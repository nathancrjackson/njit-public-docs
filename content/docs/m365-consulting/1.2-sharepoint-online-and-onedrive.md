## 1. SharePoint Hierarchy & Architecture
Modern SharePoint utilizes a "flat" architecture to ensure scalability and ease of governance, moving away from the legacy subsite model.
*   **Tenant:** The top-level boundary for the entire SharePoint service.
*   **Hub Sites:** The logical "connective tissue" used to group related site collections. Hubs provide shared navigation, consistent branding, and a unified search scope.
*   **Site Collections:** The primary units of administration. Each site collection is an independent boundary for permissions, storage quotas, and features.
*   **Document Libraries:** Containers within a site for storing files. These can have their own unique settings, including default sensitivity labels and versioning controls.
*   **Folders & Files:** The individual items stored within libraries. Item-level permissions are possible but discouraged due to administrative overhead.

## 2. Site Governance & External Collaboration
This section outlines how sites are provisioned and how data is shared outside the organizational boundary.
*   **Site Types:**
    *   **Team Sites:** These are backed by an M365 Group and include a shared inbox, calendar, and Teams integration. They are designed for internal collaboration.
    *   **Communication Sites:** These are not group-connected. They are intended for broad broadcasting, such as an Intranet homepage.
*   **Storage Management:** Administrators manage tenant-level limits and should configure site-level storage quotas to prevent a single site from exhausting the entire tenant allocation.
*   **External Sharing Hierarchy:** The tenant-level sharing setting acts as a "ceiling". Site-level settings can be more restrictive but never more permissive than the tenant setting.
    *   *Hierarchy:* `Anyone` > `New and Existing Guests` > `Existing Guests` > `Only People in Your Organization`.
*   **Entra External ID (B2B) Integration:** Integration should be enabled so guests receive a seamless authentication experience via Entra ID rather than legacy SharePoint accounts.
*   **Access Expiration:** Organizations can enforce guest access expiration policies (e.g., 90 days) at the tenant level to ensure periodic review of external access.

## 3. Permissions & Access Control
*   **M365 Group Permissions:** Team sites rely on the underlying M365 Group (Owners/Members) for primary access.
*   **Legacy Groups:** Avoid using SharePoint Groups (Visitors/Members/Owners) on group-connected sites unless specific granular access is required outside the group membership.
*   **Broken Inheritance:** Discourage breaking inheritance at the folder/file level at scale, as it impacts performance and creates management complexity. If a sub-folder requires unique permissions, consider a separate Document Library or Site.
*   **Access Requests:** Ensure request emails are routed to active owners to prevent requests from being sent to orphaned accounts.

## 4. OneDrive for Business (Enterprise Management)
*   **Known Folder Move (KFM):** Essential for device backup, KFM redirects Desktop, Documents, and Pictures to OneDrive via Intune or Group Policy.
*   **Sync Client Troubleshooting:** Common issues include file path limits (256/400 characters), invalid characters, or conflicting file locks. Sync health can be monitored via the SharePoint admin center.
*   **User Offboarding:** When an account is deleted, the OneDrive retention period begins (default 30 days). By default, the user's manager is granted access to the data for salvage before final deletion.

## 5. Essential PowerShell Cmdlets (PnP PowerShell)
*PnP PowerShell is the industry standard for SharePoint Online management, preferred over the native SharePoint Online Management Shell.*
*   **Connection:** `Connect-PnPOnline -Url [https://tenant-admin.sharepoint.com](https://tenant-admin.sharepoint.com) -Interactive`
*   **Site Management:**
    *   `Get-PnPTenantSite`: Lists all sites.
    *   `Set-PnPTenantSite -Url "[https://tenant.sharepoint.com/sites/Target](https://tenant.sharepoint.com/sites/Target)" -SharingCapability ExistingExternalUserSharingOnly`.
*   **Permissions:**
    *   `Get-PnPGroup`.
    *   `Add-PnPUserToGroup -LoginName "user@domain.com" -Identity "Site Owners"`.
*   **OneDrive Management:**
    *   `Request-SPOPersonalSite -UserEmails user@domain.com`: Pre-provisions a OneDrive site before the user logs in for the first time.
